The 2022 Federal Election for Dummies

If you're reading this, you've probably turned 18 recently. Now comes your first big test of adulthood – voting.

In Australia, voting is compulsory, mainly because it's the only way to force most of the country to get off their arse and actually do it. Before compulsory voting was introduced in the 1920s, turnout would be as low as 20%, because our country is full of lazy sods who won't do anything until threatened.

Anyways, here is the guide that I've written for my friends to understand how all of this shit works. Hopefully, this will do more informing than confusing. The idea is that by the end of this guide, you'll:

  • understand how your vote is used to elect the government;
  • know which division you're in, and where and when to vote;
  • be able to fill out the ballot paper;
  • know who's running in your division and state; and
  • be able to make your own informed ranking of the candidates to take to the polling centre.
