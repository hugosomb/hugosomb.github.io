2. Getting started with voting

Can I vote?

You can, if you're:

  • an Australian citizen, or under certain circumstances, a British subject; and
  • 18 years old or older.

Registering to vote

You need to be enrolled to vote. To check if you are, go to aec.gov.au/check. It'll ask you for your name and address.

If it can't find your details, you most likely aren't enrolled. Go to aec.gov.au/enrol.

If you've moved recently, you might still be enrolled under your old address. If you've lived at your new address for more than a month, you should update your details at aec.gov.au/update.

Side note – when you enrol with the A.E.C., it also automatically enrols you with your state's own electoral commission, so you don't have to worry about registering for state elections.

Which division am I in?

When you check your enrolment using the A.E.C. enrolment checker, or when you enrol with them, they should tell you which division you are in.

For more information on your division, such as vote margins, historical data, candidates, and demographics, the Guardian's seat explorer is a great tool to have. If you don't know your division, the seat explorer can also use your browser location to find it.

Where and when do I vote?

The election will be held on Saturday 21 May.

The A.E.C. hasn't released its list of polling centres yet. Once it becomes available, it will be linked here.

You can also vote early if you can't make it to a polling station on election day. A link to the list of early voting centres will likewise be posted here once it is released.
